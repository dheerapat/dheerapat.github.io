Who am I

Posted on Jul 13, 2024

About Myself

Hi, internet wanderer. My name is Dheeto. I am an ex-pharmacist currently working as a software engineer in Bangkok, Thailand.

You might wonder, “How come?” I ended up in software engineering. Well, let me explain it to you.

First Few Years of My Career

I graduated from pharmacy school in early 2020, the year that COVID-19 hit. The healthcare industry wasn’t affected that much, so I could still find a job pretty easily and worked as a laboratory technician for almost three years.

I chose to be a laboratory technician because, despite having a Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm. D.) degree that allows me to dispense dangerous medicine to patients, I felt like working in a hospital or drug store was my kryptonite. People management, sales targets, and customer satisfaction were things I didn’t like. So, I preferred to work as a lab rat instead.

Burnout

Time went by. Two years passed, and then I felt like I wasn’t contributing anything to the world. My job was a day-to-day routine: working as per the standard operation procedures and going home after office hours. But something in my head kept telling me, “Is this the life you want? Do you want to stay like this for the rest of your life?” And I said, “Hell no.”

I have always been passionate about technology and computer science, especially space-related computer science. So, around the end of 2022, I decided I wanted a different job and started learning a new domain of interest. I signed up for many online courses like Codecademy, LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, and many more until I found a hidden gem, freeCodeCamp.

The Day When It All Started

I took a back-end development course on this website, and it changed me forever. Many people with self-taught experience will know that when you start learning with online courses, you will reach a point called ’tutorial hell,’ where you can’t start your own project without any tutorial video or example. But after I finished this course from freeCodeCamp, I became a somewhat baby-level junior developer. I could read documentation on various frameworks, start my own projects without any video tutorials, and fix problems that occurred just by reading documentation and GitHub issues.

Around this time, I finally secured a new job as a junior back-end developer in a consulting firm. I started to work as part of a team for the first time. Everything was new to me. I learned agile, sprint, scrum, and many topics that I hadn’t heard of before. I mainly developed a CRUD system with a REST API, and I was getting good at it. Little did I know, there was so much more to learn in the software development world. That’s right, I had reached the peak of ‘Mt. Stupid.’

Valley of Despair

I noticed that the work I delivered to customers or even managers was very hard to debug and very unstructured, which also made it hard to modify, improve, or fix. I often had to rewrite whole modules from scratch. I didn’t have many friends to talk with or ask how I could improve because everyone in the company was also at a junior level, and the manager didn’t have much time to nitpick our work. So, I started looking elsewhere to satisfy my curiosity—a place where I could both work and learn how to ’engineer software,’ not just develop it.

TDD, DDD, EDA

After almost a year at the consulting firm, I finally found a new place to work. This time, it was a corporate company with its own product, and I worked as part of a team to develop an application to help maintain this product for customers.

This place introduced me to domain-driven design philosophy, and I started to write code more beautifully. I learned how to engineer programs cleanly, implemented test-driven development in real work, and learned the powerful concept of event-driven architecture, which helps decouple many parts of the program, making it easier to scale or debug.

I am so happy that I chose to join this place.

A Long Way Ahead

My journey as a software engineer has just begun. There is so much left to learn, and I am eager to do so. I created this blog to share my experiences with others and for myself as well. Until next time.
